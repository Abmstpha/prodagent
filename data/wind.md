# Wind energy

Wind turbines convert the kinetic energy of moving air into electricity. Global
installed wind capacity reached about 1,017 GW by the end of 2023, of which roughly
75 GW is offshore. China, the United States and Germany lead installed capacity.

Modern onshore turbines are typically rated between 3 MW and 6 MW, while offshore
turbines reach 8 MW to 15 MW per unit. Onshore wind farms achieve capacity factors of
25% to 45%; offshore farms reach 40% to 60% because winds at sea are stronger and
steadier. The global weighted-average LCOE was about $0.033 per kWh for onshore wind
in 2023 — the lowest of all electricity sources — and about $0.081 per kWh for
offshore wind, which costs more to build and maintain.

Wind supplied about 7.8% of global electricity in 2023. Onshore wind is cheap and fast
to deploy but faces siting opposition and variable output; offshore wind offers higher
and steadier output at a higher capital cost. Grid operators handle wind variability
with forecasting, interconnection and storage.
