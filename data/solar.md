# Solar photovoltaic energy

Solar photovoltaic (PV) systems convert sunlight directly into electricity using
semiconductor cells. Global installed solar PV capacity reached about 1,419 GW at the
end of 2023, after a record 346 GW was added during 2023 alone, making solar the
fastest-growing source of electricity in history. China hosts the largest share of
installed capacity, followed by the European Union and the United States.

Commercial solar panels have a typical module efficiency between 15% and 23%. Because
sunlight is intermittent, solar plants have a capacity factor of roughly 10% to 25%,
depending on latitude and climate. The global weighted-average levelised cost of
electricity (LCOE) for utility-scale solar PV was about $0.044 per kWh in 2023,
a fall of nearly 90% since 2010, which makes new solar the cheapest source of new
electricity in most countries.

Utility-scale plants are the largest segment, but rooftop systems on homes and
businesses account for roughly 40% of installed capacity. Solar power produced about
5.5% of global electricity in 2023. The main limitations are intermittency, the need
for storage or backup capacity at night, and land use for large ground-mounted farms.
